## Course Materials

The required casebook for this course is available for purchase at the UBC Bookstore:

- Required: Kent Roach, Benjamin Berger, Emma Cunliffe and James Stribopoulos, *Criminal Law and Procedure: Cases and Materials*, 12th ed. (Toronto: Emond Montgomery, 2020).  (“Casebook”).
- Optional: Marie Henein, *2017 Martin’s Annual Criminal Code* (Toronto: Thomson Reuters, 2016). ISBN 978-0-88804-936-0. 

An un-annotated Criminal Code is available for free online at [http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/C-46](http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/C-46){:target=" \_blank"}.

Additional readings are provided online throughout the course.

- The Midterm Examination will **cover Units 1 through 4**.  It will be ==**closed-book**== and written online using Proctorio software. **Students will be permitted to have 5 pages of typed and/or handwritten notes with them during the exam**. The pages may be 8.5 x 11 inches in size (standard letter) and the notes may fill both side of each of the 5 pages.
- Students will **not** be permitted to access the internet during the exam, or to access any electronic sources such as the course website, electronically kept notes, or digital/hardcopy textbooks.

- The Final Examination will **cover Units 1 through 7**.
