# MEMORANDUM

**FROM:** Academic Admissions Strategy Advisor

**SUBJECT:** Comprehensive Application Strategy for Engineering, Computer Science, and Sciences Programs in AB (UofA), BC, and ON

### 1. The Core Challenge & Academic Framework
Because you are starting your Alberta high school studies in the second half of Grade 11, you will only have **1.5 years of Canadian high school data** by graduation. 

Universities evaluate applications using **5 specific Grade 12 (30-level) courses** to calculate your admission average. Because your time in Alberta is short, you must coordinate with your high school guidance counselor on day one to fast-track your prerequisites.

#### Essential Course Tracks (Alberta Curriculum)
*   **Engineering:** English 30-1, Math 30-1 (Pre-Calculus), Math 31 (Calculus), Chemistry 30, and Physics 30.
*   **Computer Science & General Sciences:** English 30-1, Math 30-1, Math 31 (highly recommended), and two senior sciences (chosen from Chemistry 30, Physics 30, or Biology 30).

> ⚠️ **Action Item:** You must complete Grade 11 math/sciences in your arrival semester so you can take the mandatory 30-level sequences in Grade 12. Have your previous international transcripts formally evaluated by your Alberta school immediately to secure your **Alberta Student Number (ASN)**.

### 2. Provincial Portals & Transcripts
You will navigate different application systems depending on where you apply, with varying rules for how your grades are shared:

*   **Alberta (University of Alberta):** You will apply via the [ApplyAlberta Portal](https://applyalberta.ca). The system automatically transfers your official high school transcripts directly to the UofA, completely **free of charge**, using your ASN.
*   **British Columbia (e.g., UBC, SFU):** You will apply via the [EducationPlannerBC (EPBC)](https://educationplannerbc.ca) portal.
*   **Ontario (e.g., University of Toronto, Waterloo):** You will apply via the [Ontario Universities' Application Centre (OUAC)](https://ouac.on.ca) under the **Undergraduate Application** stream.
*   **Out-of-Province Transcript Process:** For BC and ON schools, data sharing is not automated. You must log into your [Alberta myPass account](https://public.education.alberta.ca/PASI/mypass/welcome) during Grade 12 and pay a manual processing fee to have Alberta Education mail your official transcripts to those specific institutions.

### 3. Critical Deadlines Timeline
Your earliest window to apply is **October 1 of your Grade 12 year**, when all national application portals open.

`[Oct 1: Portals Open] ---> [Dec 1: UBC Early Deadline] ---> [Jan 15: ON Deadlines] ---> [Mar 1: UofA Deadline]`

*   **October 1:** Portals open. Create your accounts on ApplyAlberta, OUAC, and EPBC.
*   **December 1:** **UBC Early Admission Deadline**. If you want early consideration in BC, your application must be submitted by this date.
*   **January 15:** **Standard Deadline** for major programs at Ontario universities (like the University of Toronto and Waterloo).
*   **Late January:** Deadline for **Supplemental Profiles**. Ontario competitive programs require a mandatory "Admissions Information Form" (AIF) or video interviews. UofA Engineering and Sciences generally do *not* require supplemental profiles; admissions are purely grade-based.
*   **March 1:** **University of Alberta Application Deadline**. Applications for the Fall term close on this day.
*   **January – February:** Log into your university portals to self-report first-semester Grade 12 midterm marks for early conditional offers. 

### 4. English Language Proficiency (ELP) Requirements
This is a critical hurdle for your specific timeline. Because you will only have 1.5 years of high school in Canada, **you will not qualify for standard English waivers** at top BC and Ontario institutions, though UofA offers slightly more flexibility.

*   **Out-of-Province (UBC & UofT):** Both strictly require **3 to 4 consecutive years** of full-time study in a recognized English-language school system to waive testing requirements. 
*   **In-Province (University of Alberta):** UofA requires **3 consecutive years** of full-time study in Canada for an automatic waiver. However, UofA will automatically waive your ELP requirement if you achieve a final blended grade of **65% or higher in Alberta English Language Arts 30-1**.
*   **Strategy:** Because your English 30-1 diploma mark won't be finalized until January or June of Grade 12, you should treat an English proficiency test as mandatory for your out-of-province applications. Sit for the **IELTS Academic** (Target: Overall 6.5+, no band below 6.0) in the **Fall of your Grade 12 year**.

### 5. Study Permit Modifications (In case PR is not yet granted)
*   **High School vs. University:** Your current Canadian study permit is coded for secondary education. It **cannot** be used to attend a university. 
*   **Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL):** Under Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) rules, any student moving from high school to a post-secondary program must get a PAL. 
*   **The Process:** You do not apply for the PAL yourself. Once you receive and accept an offer from UofA, a BC, or an ON university and pay your tuition deposit, **that specific university will apply to its respective provincial government for your PAL** and issue it to you so you can submit your updated study permit application to the IRCC.
