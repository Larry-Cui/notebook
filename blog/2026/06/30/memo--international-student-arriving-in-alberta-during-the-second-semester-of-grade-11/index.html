    MEMORANDUM

    TO: International Student Arriving in Alberta During the Second Semester of Grade 11
    FROM: Academic Admissions Strategy Advisor
    DATE: June 30, 2026
    SUBJECT: Comprehensive Admissions Strategy for Engineering, Computer Science, and Science Programs in Alberta, British Columbia, and Ontario

    Executive Summary

    Beginning Alberta high school studies during the second semester of Grade 11 creates several unique challenges, but it does not materially disadvantage a student applying to competitive Canadian universities if managed properly.

    Canadian universities regularly evaluate transfer applicants and students with mixed international and Canadian educational backgrounds. Admissions officers are generally concerned with three issues:

    1. Whether the applicant has completed all required prerequisite courses;
    2. Whether the applicant's academic performance is competitive for admission; and
    3. Whether the applicant has demonstrated the ability to succeed in a Canadian academic environment.

    The fact that only 1.5 years of Alberta high school records are available is normally not problematic by itself. The principal risks instead involve course sequencing, delayed completion of prerequisites, and the availability of sufficient Grade 12 academic evidence during the admission cycle.

    1. The Core Challenge: Arriving During Grade 11

    Because you will begin your Alberta studies during the second semester of Grade 11, your university application will contain academic records from two educational systems:

    Academic Period Source of Academic Record
    Grade 11 Semester 1 Previous international school
    Grade 11 Semester 2 Alberta high school
    Grade 12 Alberta high school

    Admissions offices routinely review applications containing multiple educational systems and generally do not consider this unusual.

    The more significant challenge is ensuring that your previous studies align with Alberta prerequisite requirements so that your progression into Grade 12 courses is not delayed.

    2. How Admissions Offices Typically View Mid-Year Grade 11 Transfers

    Many students worry that having only one semester of Alberta Grade 11 grades will negatively affect their applications.

    In practice, admissions officers rarely view the absence of a full Canadian Grade 11 transcript as a weakness.

    Instead, admissions officers typically ask:

    • Have all prerequisite courses been completed?
    • Are the grades sufficiently competitive?
    • Has the student adapted successfully to the Canadian educational system?

    A successful transition often creates a positive narrative rather than a negative one.

    For example, an admissions officer reviewing the following record may consider it favourably:

    Course International Grade Alberta Grade
    Mathematics 88% 95%
    Physics 86% 94%
    Chemistry 87% 93%

    Such results demonstrate successful academic adjustment and strong performance after arriving in Canada.

    3. The Main Risk: Course Sequencing

    For Engineering, Computer Science, and Science programs, the principal concern is not the missing semester of Canadian grades but the compressed timeline for completing prerequisites.

    Engineering

    Typical prerequisite courses include:

    • English Language Arts 30-1
    • Mathematics 30-1
    • Mathematics 31
    • Chemistry 30
    • Physics 30

    Computer Science and General Sciences

    Typical prerequisite courses include:

    • English Language Arts 30-1
    • Mathematics 30-1
    • Mathematics 31 (strongly recommended)

    • Two senior science courses selected from:

    • Chemistry 30

    • Physics 30
    • Biology 30

    Because Alberta schools generally require prerequisite completion before enrollment in senior courses, delays in receiving transfer credit evaluations may postpone access to required Grade 12 courses.

    Accordingly, the first priority upon arrival should be:

    1. Transcript assessment and placement evaluation;
    2. Determination of Alberta course equivalencies; and
    3. Immediate enrollment in all required prerequisite courses.

    4. Alberta Transcript Evaluation and the Alberta Student Number

    Upon registration at your Alberta high school, submit all previous academic records for evaluation immediately.

    This process serves two important functions:

    • determining course equivalencies and placement; and
    • activating your Alberta Student Number (ASN).

    The ASN becomes your permanent educational identifier within Alberta and is required for transcript transmission and university applications.

    5. Application Systems and Transcript Transmission

    Alberta Institutions

    Applications to Alberta universities are generally submitted through ApplyAlberta.

    For Alberta high school students, official transcripts are transmitted electronically through Alberta Education using the student's ASN.

    British Columbia Institutions

    Applications are generally submitted through EducationPlannerBC.

    Ontario Institutions

    Applications are generally submitted through the Ontario Universities' Application Centre (OUAC).

    Transcript Requests for Out-of-Province Universities

    British Columbia and Ontario universities generally do not automatically receive Alberta high school records.

    Students should therefore use Alberta's myPass system during Grade 12 to authorize transcript delivery to each institution individually.

    7. The Effect on Early Admission

    This is one area where late Grade 11 arrival can create a modest disadvantage.

    Students who completed all of Grade 11 in Alberta may already have completed several Grade 12 courses by October of Grade 12, including:

    • Mathematics 30-1;
    • Chemistry 30; and
    • Physics 30.

    By contrast, a mid-Year-11 transfer student may have fewer completed Grade 12 courses available for evaluation during early admission rounds.

    Consequently:

    • admission decisions may arrive later;
    • fewer early offers may be issued; and
    • some universities may wait for additional Grade 12 results.

    This generally affects timing rather than admission outcomes.

    Receiving an offer in February or March instead of November or December is common for transfer students and should not be interpreted negatively.

    8. The Treatment of International Grades

    Universities do not ignore the grades obtained before arriving in Canada.

    Instead, they evaluate all available academic evidence.

    If international grades are strong, they support the application.

    If international grades are weaker, universities may rely more heavily on subsequent Alberta performance as additional results become available.

    For example:

    Course International Grade Alberta Grade
    Physics 72% 94%
    Mathematics 75% 95%

    Strong Alberta performance often demonstrates academic growth and adaptation and can significantly improve the overall assessment of an applicant.

    9. English Language Proficiency Requirements

    Students arriving during Grade 11 frequently do not satisfy the automatic English language exemption requirements imposed by some Canadian universities.

    Because exemption rules vary by institution and change periodically, applicants should assume that an English proficiency examination may be required.

    The recommended strategy is to complete an approved examination during the fall semester of Grade 12.

    Recommended target scores:

    • IELTS Academic: overall score of 6.5 or higher;
    • no individual band below 6.0.

    Obtaining qualifying scores early removes uncertainty from the application process.

    10. Strategies to Strengthen the Application

    Students arriving midway through Grade 11 can significantly strengthen their applications through the following measures.

    Complete Prerequisites as Early as Possible

    The earlier Grade 12 prerequisite courses are completed, the stronger the application becomes.

    Priority courses include:

    • Mathematics 30-1;
    • Chemistry 30; and
    • Physics 30.

    Complete Mathematics 31

    Although not always formally required, calculus preparation is highly valued in Engineering and Computer Science admissions.

    Demonstrate Strong Academic Improvement

    Admissions officers frequently view upward academic trends favourably.

    Strong Alberta grades can demonstrate:

    • adaptability;
    • resilience; and
    • readiness for university studies.

    Complete English Testing Early

    Completing IELTS or another approved examination during the fall semester of Grade 12 removes uncertainty surrounding language requirements.

    Use Supplementary Applications Strategically

    Programs requiring interviews, personal profiles, or supplementary essays provide opportunities to explain:

    • international educational background;
    • relocation to Canada;
    • adaptation to a new educational system; and
    • multilingual and multicultural experiences.

    11. Study Permit Considerations

    If permanent residence has not yet been granted at the time university studies begin, immigration planning becomes important.

    Students transitioning from secondary school to university should ensure that their study authorization permits attendance at a post-secondary institution.

    Most new post-secondary study permit applications currently require a Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL).

    Universities generally provide instructions regarding the PAL process after an admission offer has been accepted and any required tuition deposit has been paid.

    12. Overall Assessment

    From an admissions perspective, arriving in Alberta during the second semester of Grade 11 is generally viewed as a neutral factor rather than a negative one.

    Admissions offices are accustomed to evaluating applicants with mixed educational backgrounds and international experience.

    The situation only becomes problematic if the late arrival results in:

    • missing prerequisite courses;
    • delayed access to Grade 12 subjects; or
    • insufficient academic evidence during the admission cycle.

    If these issues are addressed successfully, a student who arrives midway through Grade 11 remains fully competitive for admission to Engineering, Computer Science, and Science programs across Alberta, British Columbia, and Ontario.

    Indeed, a record demonstrating successful transition into the Canadian educational system and strong academic achievement after arrival may strengthen an application rather than weaken it.
